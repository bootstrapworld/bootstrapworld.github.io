The Design Recipe (Slope/Intercept 2)

Directions: The cost of a ride is a starting price of $2.50, plus $1.50/mile. Use the Design Recipe to write a function rideshare, that takes in a number of miles and produces the cost of that right.

Contract and Purpose Statement

Every contract has three parts…​

rideshare::Number->Number

Consumes miles, multiplies by 1.50 and then adds 2.50 to produce the cost

Examples

Write some examples, then circle and label what changes…​

examples:

__rideshare (0)__ is 2.5 + (​0 * 1.5​)

__rideshare (3)__ is 2.5 + (​3 * 1.5​)

end

Definition

Write the definition, giving variable names to all your input values…​

fun rideshare(miles):

__2.5 + (​miles * 1.5​)

end

Directions: Manhattan Country School is planning to take their 7th & 8th graders to an exhibit at the Museum of the City of New York and stop for tamales on the way back to school. The museum charges a flat fee of $175 for a school group and tamales are $2 each. Use the Design Recipe to write a function field-trip that takes in the number of students, n, and calculates the total cost of the field trip.

Contract and Purpose Statement

Every contract has three parts…​

field-trip::Number->Number

Consumes the number of students, doubles it, and adds 175.

Examples

Write some examples, then circle and label what changes…​

examples:

__field-trip (20)__ is (​2 * 20​) + 175

__field-trip (35)__ is (​2 * 35​) + 175

end

Definition

Write the definition, giving variable names to all your input values…​

fun field-trip(n):

__(​2 * n​) + 175

end
