Rocket Height Challenges

1 Can you make the rocket fly faster?

multiply seconds by a number larger than 7: heightseconds = 20 × seconds

2 Can you make the rocket fly slower?

multiply seconds by a number smaller than 7: heightseconds = 2 × seconds

3 Can you make the rocket sink down instead of fly up?

multiply seconds by a negative number: heightseconds = -7 × seconds

4 Can you make the rocket accelerate over time, so that it moves faster the longer it flies?

The formula for acceleration is quadratic: heightseconds = 7 × seconds^2.

5 Can you make the rocket blast off and then land again?

A parabola with a negative coefficient, shifted right by subtracting from the squared term and up by adding a constant: heightseconds = -(seconds - 10)^2 + 100

6 Can you make the rocket blast off, reach a maximum height of exactly 1000 meters, and then land?

In the previous solution, the constant we add is the square of the constant we subtract from seconds. To reach a height of 1000: heightseconds = -(seconds - √1000)^2 + 1000

7 Can you make the rocket blast off, reach a maximum height of exactly 1000 meters, and then land after exactly 100 seconds?

A good challenge for a unit on quadratics! heightseconds = 0.4 × seconds^2 + 40seconds

8 Can you make the rocket fly to the edge of the the universe?

What’s the inverse function of log? Exponential

Use any number for the base and seconds as the exponent: heightseconds = 7^seconds
