Do The Examples Have the Same Contracts?

For each pair of Examples below, decide whether the two examples have the same contract. If they do, fill in the contract in the space provided. If not, write a few words explaining how you know their contracts aren’t the same.

1 no match (the range types are different)

(EXAMPLE (mystery 30) (* 30 50)) (EXAMPLE (mystery 10) (text "Welcome!" 10 "darkgreen"))

2 ; mystery :: Number, Number ‑> Number

(EXAMPLE (mystery 30 40) (- 40 (* 2 30))) (EXAMPLE (mystery 10 15) (- 15 (* 2 10)))

3 no match (the domain types are different)

(EXAMPLE (mystery "New York") (text "New York" 20 "red")) (EXAMPLE (mystery 20) (text "New York" 20 "red"))

4 no match (the domain types are in different orders)

(EXAMPLE (mystery "green" 32) (circle 32 "outline" "green")) (EXAMPLE (mystery 18 "green") (circle 18 "outline" "green"))

5 no match (the domains have different numbers of arguments)

(EXAMPLE (mystery 6 9 10) (/ 6 (+ 9 10))) (EXAMPLE (mystery 3 7) (/ 3 (+ 7 10)))

6 ; mystery :: String, String ‑> Image

(EXAMPLE (mystery "red" "blue") (text "blue" 25 "red")) (EXAMPLE (mystery "purple" "Go Team!") (text "Go Team!" 25 "purple"))
